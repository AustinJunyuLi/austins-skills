# Research Ideation

## Overview

Front door for research thinking before the work is fully specified. This skill covers open-ended idea generation, structured hypothesis formation, interview-style specification, scenario analysis, and multi-perspective challenge.

## Use This For

- generating research questions from a topic
- turning observations into testable hypotheses
- stress-testing a proposed project
- interviewing yourself into a sharper design
- exploring alternative mechanisms or scenario branches
- challenging assumptions before committing

## Do Not Use This For

- searching the literature: use `research-discovery`
- reviewing a completed paper or design: use `research-review`
- implementation planning for software changes: use `superpowers:brainstorming` or `superpowers:writing-plans`

## Internal Routing

- open-ended ideation
- structured hypothesis generation
- interview-driven scoping
- what-if and scenario analysis
- adversarial or multi-voice challenge

## Operating Rule

If the user is still deciding what to study or how to frame it, this is the front door. Once the question is defined, hand off to discovery, analysis, or writing.
